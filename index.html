Our Story

Every Dish Tells a Story

Biryani, a beloved dish in South Asia, has a rich and intriguing history. Its origins can be traced back to the Mughal Empire, which significantly influenced the South Asian subcontinent’s culinary landscape during the 16th and 17th centuries. The word “biryani” itself comes from the Persian term “birian,” meaning “fried before cooking.” This flavorful rice dish was initially introduced by the Mughals in South Asian subcontinent. There are various regional variations of biryani, including Sindhi Biryani, Punjabi Biryani, Bohri Biryani, Afghani Biryani, and the traditional Mughlai Biryani or Hyderabadi-style Biryani. Each locality adds its unique flavor and traditions to this beloved culinary delight. Our Executive chief has years of culinary expertise in finest hotels across South Asia and Ireland, mastering and excelling the crafts of this crown Asian Cuisine "Biryani".
